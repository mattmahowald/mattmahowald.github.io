Beyond Academics

In-School Activities

These concepts are not limited to the classroom. On the schoolgrounds, there are still many places linguistic diversity has an impact. One of the most important, especially for young students, is on the playground. As children, we learn much of our social skills from play. The playground is an opportunity for us to practice interactions with others in a safe environment. This is an opportunity for teachers and administrators to apply this new pedagogy to instill the values CSP promotes. Through advocating for inclusive games that celebrate heritage, students will learn the ability to appreciate their own and other's cultures, as well as gain a respect for those different from them.

Thought Questions

      What kinds of games and activities can you think of that promote sharing your culture or other cultures? How can you think about integrating students into these games, and what if they are hesitant at first?
      Role play: what might you do if you face this situation? A student tells you he doesn't want to play with another student because the other student does not share the same color skin.
      At a parent-teacher conference, what can you do to encourage your student's parents to increase the home's recognition of linguistic diversity?
