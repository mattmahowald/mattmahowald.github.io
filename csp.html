Culturally Sustaining Pedagogy

A New Approach

Culturally sustaining pedagogy is an evolution of what has been deemed culturally responsive or culturally relevant pedagogy. The terms “responsive” and “relevant” can be interpreted as too passive of an approach to value cultural and linguistic sharing across difference. Culturally sustaining pedagogy not only tolerates difference, but also celebrates it. Its explicit goal is to perpetuate and foster linguistic, literate, and cultural pluralism as part of the democratic project of schooling. This model values both within-group cultural practices (i.e. Spanish, Navajo, African American Vernacular English also known as Ebonics) and across-group cultural practices (Dominant American English in most schools in the United States).



Both of these types of practices are very important to creating a society based on multicultural pluralism. In the past, across-group cultural practices have commonly been most focused on in educational settings, sometimes at the expense of within-group practices. While culturally relevant pedagogy aims to minimize these sacrifices, culturally sustaining pedagogy makes it an active goal to value these practices. Examples for the value that such practices provide can be found in recent research that has examined how many African American students navigate identities through African American Language and Hip Hop culture, as well as how these practices inspire other young people of color, including many Latina/o and Pacific Islander youth around the world.
