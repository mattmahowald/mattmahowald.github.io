Reflection

Take a minute to reflect on what you have learned about over the course of reading this content. Then, spend some time writing down how you will work to engage more cultures in your classrooms. Come up with three concrete methods of exposing your students to these different groups.

You are the teacher representative on a search committee for a new teacher for your school. Develop a list of qualities or characteristics you would be looking for. Whold race, ethnicity, gender, or language background make a difference? Why or why not? What if the school were very culturally diverse or entirely monocultural? Would this change your answer?

Give some examples of collaboration between schools and parents that could help students achieve more in schools. Think across the board, from meetings, to curriculum, to outside of school activities such as sports, student government, and publications.

You have a student in an English classroom whose primary language is not English. He is acting out, not responding to instruction, and disrespecting peers. Considering his language differences, devise an action plan to help your student reform.
